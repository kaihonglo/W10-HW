Carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions are the primary driver of global climate change. It's widely recognised that to avoid the worst impacts of climate change, the world needs to urgently reduce emissions. But, how this responsibility is shared between regions, countries, and individuals has been an endless point of contention in international discussions. (Our World In Data, n.d.)

How have global emissions of carbon dioxide (CO2) changed over time? In this chart we see on average, the trends and change of regional emissions from the year 1990 through to 2019.

It can be seen that the European and Central Asian region are the leaders in CO2 Emission around the world, but this could be explained by their large population and land

CO2 Emissions (metric ton per capita) around the world in 2019



As shown in the map above, the countries that are among the HIGHEST CO2 Emission per capita in the year 2019 were The United States, Canada, Saudi Arabia, Australia, Oman

But those are large countries that can be easily seen on the map, when zoomed in the Middle East, it is revealed that countries like Qatar and Kuwait have even higher CO2 Emission per capita than the countries above.

Here it is shown more clearly in the Pie Chart that Europe & Central Asia take up a high percentage of the Global CO2 Emission, where it is more than 50% in the year 1990 but has since decreased.

It is also shown, that North America take up a low percentage of the Global CO2 Emission but has the greatest average region CO2 Emission, this could be due to it having a low number of countries but a large populations and land.

The visualisation on the right is a boxplot which is a chart that shows statistics on the CO2 Emission of each country

It is filtered according to region since it is somewhat unreadable for all regions

The statistics include the minimum(Min), first quartile(Q1), median(Q2), third quartile(Q3) and the maximum(Max) of CO2 emission for each country over the years

With this chart, we are able to see if a country has high CO2 Emission for many years, or if that year was an outlier .
